Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India", was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician. He served as the 11th President of India (2002-2007). Born in Ramaswami in Tamil Nadu, he studied physics and aerospace engineering. Kalam worked at ISRO and DRDO, leading India's space and missile programs. He played a key role in developing India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and guided missile programs. A Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and Bharat Ratna (1997) awardee, Kalam was known for his simplicity and vision for India's development. He authored books like "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds". Kalam emphasized education, innovation, and youth empowerment. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture in Shillong.
